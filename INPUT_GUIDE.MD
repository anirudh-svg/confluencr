# Confluencr — Input Guide

How to fill in the form to get the best possible tweets. The more specific your inputs, the more authentic the output.

---

## Brand Tone

Tone describes the *personality and emotional register* of how your brand communicates. You can combine multiple tone words.

### Tone Options

| Tone | What it means | Example brand |
|---|---|---|
| **Witty** | Clever, playful with language, likes wordplay | Zomato, Duolingo |
| **Bold** | Confident, direct, doesn't hedge | Nike, Harley Davidson |
| **Premium / Luxury** | Elevated, refined, minimal words, high impact | Apple, Rolex |
| **Friendly** | Warm, approachable, feels like a person not a brand | Innocent Drinks, Oatly |
| **Humorous** | Overtly funny, meme-aware, doesn't take itself seriously | Wendy's, Dollar Shave Club |
| **Inspirational** | Motivating, uplifting, belief-driven | Nike, Lululemon |
| **Authoritative** | Expert, credible, data-backed | McKinsey, HBR |
| **Minimalist** | Few words, high signal, lets the product speak | Apple, Muji |
| **Edgy** | Pushes boundaries, provocative, contrarian | Liquid Death, Supreme |
| **Empathetic** | Emotionally aware, listens first, human-centred | Headspace, Calm |
| **Playful** | Light, fun, doesn't over-explain | M&Ms, Skittles |
| **Conversational** | Casual, like texting a friend | Slack, Notion |
| **Educational** | Teaches, explains, breaks things down | Byju's, Khan Academy |
| **Urgency-driven** | Creates FOMO, time-sensitive, action-first | Swiggy, BookMyShow |
| **Chaotic / Unhinged** | Deliberately absurd, Gen Z humour | Duolingo, Ryanair |

### How to write it in the form

Instead of just one word, combine 2–3 for precision:

```
witty and self-aware with a chaotic edge
premium but approachable, never arrogant
bold and educational, like a smart friend who knows their stuff
warm and playful, never corporate
minimalist and confident — lets the product do the talking
```

## Campaign Objective

Tell Confluencr *what you want the tweets to do*. This shapes the mix of content styles generated.

| Objective | Best for |
|---|---|
| **Brand Awareness** | New brand, new market, want more people to know you exist |
| **Product Launch** | New product or feature dropping soon |
| **Drive Engagement** | Want replies, retweets, saves — algorithm growth |
| **Promotional / Offer** | Sale, discount, limited-time deal |
| **Community Building** | Build a loyal audience, foster belonging |
| **Thought Leadership** | Position brand/founder as an expert in the space |
| **Event Promotion** | Concert, webinar, product demo, launch event |

## Products & Services — How to Describe Them

This is the most important field. Vague input = generic tweets. Specific input = tweets that actually sound like your brand.

### Instead of this → write this

| Vague | Specific |
|---|---|
| "We sell shoes" | "Performance running shoes built for marathon runners, priced at ₹8,000, focused on cushioning and lightweight design" |
| "Food delivery app" | "10-minute grocery delivery in Bangalore and Mumbai, dark store model, 5,000+ SKUs, targeting urban millennials" |
| "EdTech platform" | "Live coding bootcamps for working professionals switching careers into tech, 6-month programs, job guarantee" |
| "Skincare brand" | "Minimalist skincare for Indian skin tones — 5-ingredient formulas, dermatologist-tested, no fragrance, ₹500–₹1,200 range" |
| "Fitness app" | "AI-powered home workout app with no equipment needed, 15-minute sessions, targets busy professionals aged 25–40" |

The more product detail you give, the more Confluencr can write tweets about *specific features, price points, and real use cases* rather than generic category statements.

## Additional Context — Power Tips

This optional field is where you can really steer the output. Use it to provide:

### 1. Audience language
Words and phrases your actual customers use:
```
Our audience says "grind" not "work hard", "collab" not "partnership",
they use "lowkey", "no cap", "it's giving" — Gen Z urban India
```

### 2. Brands you sound like (or want to sound like)
```
We want to sound like Zomato meets Apple — witty but premium
Similar tone to Notion's Twitter — calm, confident, slightly nerdy
```

### 3. What to avoid
```
Never sound corporate or use jargon like "synergy" or "leverage"
Avoid being preachy or motivational-poster style
Don't use exclamation marks — we never use them
```

### 4. Current campaign or moment
```
We just launched in Delhi — lean into the city pride angle
It's summer — focus on heat, sweat, outdoor use cases
We crossed 1 million users last week — celebratory but humble
```

### 5. Founder / brand personality notes
```
Founded by two IIT dropouts — lean into the underdog narrative
We're a women-led brand — that's central to our identity
Our mascot is a sloth — we use slow = intentional as a brand metaphor
```

| Field | Value |

| **Industry** | Food & Beverage |
